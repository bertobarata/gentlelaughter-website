# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential clients in Portugal evaluating Gentle Laughter for a specific need: a couple planning a wedding or batizado, a venue or promoter looking to book Vanessa Alves (fado), a producer needing stagehands or transfer, an events team renting radios, baias, or extintores, a production house looking for special effects and cenografia, an event organiser wanting a chamuça/wrap trailer onsite, a partner referral (auto, real estate, crédito, contabilidade), or a reader interested in the book "A noite de Célio Correia."

These visitors arrive curious but uncertain whether the same company really delivers all nine lines competently. Their job-to-be-done in the first minute: confirm relevance to their specific need, gauge professionalism, identify the right contact path. Most will browse services and only then decide whether to message via WhatsApp or the form.

## Product Purpose

A single trustworthy index for an umbrella production and services company with nine distinct lines of business. The site exists to make a wide, unconventional portfolio look intentional rather than scattered: every line is reachable in one click, none is buried in a sub-menu or footer, and the brand carries the coherence the offerings cannot carry on their own.

The home bento applies one calibrated asymmetry: 01 Organização de eventos and 02 Agenciamento de fado occupy hero and medium tiles respectively, because these two lines drive the highest-intent inbound traffic (weddings, batizados, fado bookings) and are the company's signature offer. Lines 03–08 take equal small tiles. Line 09 (the book) takes the full-width footer tile because it belongs to a different register (memoir, not service). Equal weight applies at the level of numbering, treatment and reachability; visual hierarchy reflects business reality.

Success: a visitor lands, scans the index, finds the line that applies to them in under thirty seconds, reaches a contact path with confidence, and never wonders whether they are in the right place.

## Brand Personality

Confident, considered, quietly humane. The wordmark is a delicate script paired with spaced caps, which sets the tone: this is not loud events-industry glamour, nor corporate sterility. The voice is plain, direct, Portuguese, low on adjectives. Three words: composed, capable, warm.

## Anti-references

- Events-industry cliché: gold on black, drapery, glitter, stock wedding hero photography, "luxo & glamour" headlines.
- Corporate SaaS template: navy and grey, hero metric block, stock business photography, identical-card service grids.
- Pastel kawaii or baby-shower aesthetics that the name "Gentle Laughter" might falsely invite.
- Card grids of nine identical icon + heading + paragraph tiles. The portfolio is not a shop; it is an index.

## Design Principles

1. **Index, not catalogue.** Nine lines, equal weight, presented as an editorial contents page rather than a marketing card grid. Discipline replaces decoration.
2. **Trust through restraint.** The portfolio's unusual breadth is reassured by typographic confidence and generous whitespace, not by photography or badges.
3. **Wordmark first.** The existing script logo is the strongest brand asset. Type system supports it; nothing competes with it visually.
4. **One bilingual surface for a Portuguese audience.** Copy is Portuguese (PT), professional, plain. Headings do not restate themselves.
5. **Light and dark are equals.** Both modes ship with their own logo file and are designed simultaneously, not retrofitted.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast in both modes. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: no scroll-triggered transforms or autoplay motion when set. Tap targets ≥44px on mobile. Forms labelled and keyboard-navigable. The script wordmark is decorative; the accessible name is the business name in plain text.
